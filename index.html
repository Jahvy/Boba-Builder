The Game


  • Ages 8+
  • 15 min
  • 2-5 Players

How fast can you build these menus? In this quick and addictive game, you win if you become the first player to get rid of all of your bubble tea and topping cards. Simple right? But be sure to watch out for those tricky action cards along the way. You might have to get new cards or do something crazy!

How to Play

Object of the Game: Be the first player to get rid of all your tea and topping cards!

Setup

Create your menu and deal cards

  1. Remove menu cards, shuffle the remaining cards, and deal the following face-down decks, depending on the number of players:

    • 2 players: Deal 28 cards to each player
    • 3 players: Deal 24 cards to each player
    • 4 players: Deal 20 cards to each player
    • 5 players: Deal 16 cards to each player
  2. Place the remaining cards in a face-down deck in the center of the playing area.
  3. Shuffle and randomly select one of the menu cards. Place that card face up for all other players to see.
  4. Players will pick up the top 5 cards of their personal decks into their hands.

Rules of Play

Be the first player to get rid of all your tea and topping cards!

The game begins once a selected player (usually the dealer) flips the top card from the center deck and places it face-up in the center playing area to start a face-up pile. If the card is an action card, that action will be applied to every player.

All players can then begin placing cards on the face-up pile simultaneously one at a time. Cards can be placed onto the center pile based on the following 3 rules:

  1. Tea & Topping Cards: You can place any tea or topping card that is related to the top face-up card in the center pile as shown by the game’s menu card. You cannot play the same type of tea or topping card consecutively on top of one another.
  2. Action Cards: Action cards are associated with a specific tea or topping. These cards follow the same placement rules as normal tea and topping cards. Actions must be followed immediately after action cards are played.

When a card is removed from your hand, you can pick up the top card from your personal deck. You cannot have more than 5 cards in your hand at once except when receiving new cards due to an action.

If you place the wrong card on top of another, you must take the card back into your hand and pick up an additional card from the center deck.

When no players are able to play a card, take turns flipping over the top card from the center deck and place it face-up onto the center pile.

The first player to get rid of all the player’s cards wins the game.
